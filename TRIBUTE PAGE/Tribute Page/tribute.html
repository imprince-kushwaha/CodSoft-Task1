INTRODUCTION:-

Honoring the fearless patriot, Shaheed Bhagat Singh, who played a pivotal role in India's fight for freedom. Bhagat Singh is often referred to as "Shaheed" Bhagat Singh. The word 'Shaheed' means a martyr.His courage, ideals, and sacrifices continue to inspire generations.



EARLY LIFE AND IDEALS:-

Bhagat Singh was born into a Punjabi Sikh family on 27 September 1907 in the village of Banga in the Lyallpur district of the Punjab in what was then British India and is today Pakistan; he was the second of seven children—four sons, and three daughters. His Mother's name was Vidyavati and his father was Kishan Singh Sandhu.



THE AWAKENING:-

◉Impact of the Jallianwala Bagh massacre on Bhagat Singh's young mind.
◉His growing discontent with British rule and determination to contribute to the freedom struggle.
◉Involvement in the Non-Cooperation Movement and influences of prominent leaders like Mahatma Gandhi.



THE JALLIANWALA BAGH MASSACRE:-

The Jallianwala Bagh massacre of 1919 stands as one of the most horrifying incidents in India's history under British colonial rule. The callous actions of British troops, under the command of General Reginald Dyer, resulted in the brutal killing and injuring of hundreds of innocent civilians who had gathered in the Jallianwala Bagh in Amritsar, Punjab.The sight of unarmed men, women, and children being mercilessly fired upon by British troops left an everlasting scar on Bhagat Singh's heart. The incident awakened a profound sense of injustice and a burning desire to see his nation free from the clutches of British tyranny.

THE LAHORE CONSPIRACY CASE:-

◉Assassination of James A. Scott and execution of Lala Lajpat Rai's assailants.
◉Bhagat Singh's role and eventual arrest in the Lahore Conspiracy Case.
◉Trial, courtroom speeches, and articulation of his vision for a free and just India.

DEFIANCE AND RESISTANCE:-

In 1929,Bhagat Singh,accompanied by Batukeshwar Dutt,threw two bombs into the Assembly chamber from its public gallery. Their intention was not tom kill anyone but to gain publicity.After the explosion,the young men began shouting the slogan "Inquilab Zindabad!"("Long Live The Revolution ") and threw leaflets.Then they offered themselves for arrest.

QUOTES FROM BHAGAT SINGH:-

• "They may kill me, but they cannot kill my ideas. They can crush my body, but they will not be able to crush my spirit."
• "Merciless criticism and independent thinking are the two necessary traits of revolutionary thinking."

CONCLUSION:-

• Reflection on Bhagat Singh's legacy as a symbol of courage, sacrifice, and patriotism.
• Bhagat Singh's legacy is a testament to the power of one individual's conviction to bring about change. His sacrifice continues to inspire us to stand up against injustice and fight for a better future.
• The continuing relevance of his ideals in the modern world.
• Invitation to remember and honor his memory as a national hero and an inspiration for generations to come.
